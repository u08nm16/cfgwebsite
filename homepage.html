TRANSPORT

How to travel sustainably around town.
Whilst Aberdeen is hardly known for its public transport, there are many different ways to get about easily enough if you’re in the know. In the UK, transportation is the responsible for 27% of Greenhouse gas emissions (data given from the Department for Business, Energy & Industrial Strategy).So when it comes to going green, it’s a very good place to start.
There’s a myth among students that you cannot cycle safely in Aberdeen, and whilst there is room for improvement in terms of infrastructure. This is simply not true. Data from a survey collected by students about students showed that approximately 1 in 10 of our fulltime university students cycle, meaning more than a thousand students currently cycle throughout the Granite City.

Reduce, Reuse, Recycle, and What is Zero Waste?
  1. Recycle everything you can, including food waste for agricultural compost.

  2. Re-fill your soap and laundry detergent at local shops like Foodstory and Nature’s Larder.

  3. Buy shampoo and conditioner bars - plastic free.

  4. Switch energy suppliers to a more green alternative.

  5. Don’t buy into fast-fashion, by supporting local charity and vintage shops for your clothes and accessories.

Check out this links to Foodstory for refillable foods, Nature's Larder for refillable detergents, cleaning and hygiene products, and Oliveira's plastic-free groceries in Aberdeenshire:

FOOD

Because let's be honest, you probably think about it more than your classes.

Here are some easy tips for making food shopping and preparation more eco-friendly

  • Buy your fruit and vegetables loose - it saves on plastic, particulary single-use plastics.

  • Avoid products containing palm oil, fats, and solids.Palm oil heavily contributes to deforestation. The palm oil industry plays a large role in human induced climate change as palm oil plantations have cleared some of the worlds most precious, carbon capturing forests.

  • Avoid single use plastics, there is clear labelling on the back of products with details. If there are none, it's probably non-recyclable.

  • Buy a bamboo biodegradable coffee cup and enjoy money off on your coffee!

  • Try meat-free mondays, or you're already vegetarian try dairy-free days.
